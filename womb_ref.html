Curatorial Note

The Womb and the Law

The numerous problems in the scheme of the Surrogacy Regulation Act, 2021 become apparent on a close reading of the Act. Though noble in its intent to prevent exploitation, it reinforces traditional ideas and stereotypes of the idea of a family. This is, in many cases, exclusionary, and also reduces the dignity of women's labour as her act is perceived and oriented not through her own autonomy, but through a noble, sacrifice or duty for the sake of her family.

The authors in How can Families be Imagined Beyond Kinship make this fault line explicit by arguing that the SRA legitimizes the idea that a woman has to reproduce for the good of society. That it is a cause that may be undertaken for the sake of her husband, and even her relatives, but never her own self. The SRA is most destructive in its mythic framing of women's reproductive labour as benevolent. Consequently, it invisibilises the same as actual work worthy of compensation.

We recognise that such things may be visibly apparent to a layperson with only a brief knowledge of the functioning of law. Therefore, for the first component of our project we have crafted a short infographic video that we animated ourselves from scratch. This video serves to explain the Surrogacy Regulation Act through a lens of objectivity, situating and laying out the act's background, intent, and many controversial provisions.

To add depth to this, we also engaged various stakeholders for their views of the Surrogacy Act, and their opinions on how the tensions at the Act's core could be resolved. We consulted policy drafters and scholars such as Ms. Brototi Dutta and Bagmishika Puhan to understand their perspectives. Our stakeholder consultation culminated in a podcast with Ms. Brototi Dutta, a reproductive-rights lawyer.

Based on the many insights gleaned from our conversation with Ms. Dutta and Ms. Puhan, we conducted an exercise in formally redrafting the Surrogacy Act to make it more amenable to the realities of our times, and to resolve the problems of it being underinclusive and over-regulatory in its heavy-handed approach to Surrogacy.

To understand the popular understanding of Surrogacy in India through its representation in media, we created a movie report on the films 'Mimi' and 'Chori Chori Chupke Chupke' which dive into the nuances which govern everyday life, something that the SRA glosses over. The reality implicit in the film is that the lack of formal regulation pushes such acts underground, away from the state's coercive gaze.

By: Diya Ranjith, Kunsh Jain, Jayesh Dani, Vaishnavi Karthik Prasad

Redraft Highlights

Based on insights from our stakeholder consultations, we conducted an exercise in formally redrafting the Surrogacy Act to make it more amenable to the realities of our times, resolving problems of it being underinclusive and over-regulatory.

  • Expanded Recognition: Includes all forms of domestic partnerships
  • Gender-Neutral Language: Uses inclusive terminology throughout
  • Holistic Economic Assessment: Considers informal labor and caregiving
  • Intersectional Framework: Mandates consideration of compounding factors
